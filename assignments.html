Assignment 1: Working With Sources :assignment:

Motivation

Librarian Whitney Kemble has graciously made a pair of videos for us about the Zotero reference management software. The importance of this tool should not be underestimated. Using it will save you many hours – many panicked, late-night hours – over the course of your University studies.

The videos are here:

  • Video 1
  • Video 2

You will need to login with your UTORid in order to watch them.

More valuable resources on research skills can be found in these search tutorials and the History research guide. You will likely find these valuable for this assignment a well as subsequent ones.

Description

In this assignment, you will use the bibliographic skills demonstrated in the videos to locate five scholarly sources. You will then write a single, coherent paragraph in which you cite all five sources, and include a Bibliography with full bibliographic information for each source.

The sources and the paragraph should be relevant to one of the course lectures you have heard. Your choices are:

  • What is Technology?
  • Ancient Agriculture
  • Technologies of Ancient Warfare
  • The Writing on the Wall
  • Technologies of the Sea
  • Technologies of Administration

Criteria

  • Your sources should all be scholarly sources. For a definition of a scholarly source, see this link. They should also be relevant to the subject matter at hand, and must not already be in the group Zotero library for this class.
  • Your paragraph should reference all five sources and very briefly summarize their contributions to your understanding of the topic. It should be clear, concise, and demonstrate your grasp of the subject matter. Length: ~150 +/- 50 w0rds.
  • Your citations and bibliography should use either the MLA or the Chicago (Notes) style. You should use Zotero to format them perfectly. No partial credit is given for this part of the assignment.

Assignment 2: Article Review :assignment:

Summary

Use the research and bibliographic skills you developed in assignment 1 to write a short (3pp, ~800 words) essay engaging with a course reading. Develop an argument, give convincing evidence, and cite at least two additional scholarly sources which also cite the article you have chosen.

Details

This is a lightly-modified “reading response paper”. Your job is to respond to an author’s arguments, either by disagreeing with a point they make, or by extending or enhancing their argument, or, sometimes, all of the above. For this particular assignment, you should also engage with additional sources by authors already engaged in a conversation with the source you are reading.

Your essay should present a clear and compelling argument about the strengths and/or weaknesses of the original source. Your argument should be supported by evidence. Present evidence from both the original reading, and the additional sources you are using. Write in a clear style, and bear in mind the intellectual frameworks that you have learned thus far in the course.

Use Modern Language Association (9th edition) for all citations and bibliography.

Readings

Respond to one of the following readings in your essay:

  • (Marx 2010)
  • (Franklin 1999)
  • (Gabrial 2023)
  • “Landscaping the World”, in (Scott 2017)
  • (Thompson 1967)

Your additional sources should cite the reading you choose. A Google Scholar citation search can help you in your search.

Assignment 3: Technology Timeline :assignment:

Summary

Choose an everyday technology that forms part of your daily life. It can be ubiquitous (forks, laptop computers, paper clips) or rare. Using TimelineJS, create a timeline identifying important dates in this technology’s history. Then write a brief (900-1100 word) essay about that history.

Details

This is a short research paper accompanied by a simple visualization. Let’s talk about the research paper first, and then about the visualization.

A research paper is a chance to make and support an argument about a historical phenomenon. Your paper should express – and support! – a point of view about its subject. Why is this technology interesting? What “affordances” does it have – what kinds of action does it encourage or discourage? You will need to answer questions like these. While brief, your paper should have an introduction, a conclusion, and several body paragraphs, each of which makes a point and provides evidence for it.

Research Requirements:

  • Use at least 5 scholarly sources (academic books, journal articles, or equivalent)
  • Sources must be relevant to your technology’s history and cultural impact
  • Go beyond basic encyclopedia entries or Wikipedia for your core evidence
  • Consider primary sources where available (patents, advertisements, user manuals, etc.)

Choosing a Technology Think about the technologies in your life. Where do they come form? What effects do they have on your life? What broader effects have they had in the world? Make a list of two or three or five or ten that are of interest to you. [note: doubtless it is frustrating that I am not listing anything specific. But if I do, then 50% of you will write about one of my examples.]

Then confirm that there is extant scholarly literature about the history of the technologies you are considering. Skim a few articles. Soon you will find some facts and arguments that genuinely interest you. This is a sign that the topic is a good match for you; choose that subject.

Timeline Requirements: TimelineJS is a simple tool for making timelines. Before starting, think about this “information technology” and its role in transmitting/enhancing an argument. Your timeline should:

  • Contain at least 8 entries spanning your technology’s development
  • Include pictures in at least 5 entries (ensure images use Creative Commons licensing)
  • Support and complement your essay’s argument
  • The library has kindly provided a timeline module here which contains many details about timelines, including helpful guidelines about fair use of images. (Quercus link unavailable during outage) has specific instructions about how to use TimelineJS. Read this carefully. Please note: this module may be revised slightly in the next day or two.

Format Requirements:

  • Citations in Chicago style (either Notes-Bibliography or Author-Date)
  • Include a complete bibliography
  • Submit both your essay and a link to your published TimelineJS timeline
